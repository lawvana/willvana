Common Questions

What people ask
before they start.

Do I need a trust, a will, or both?
Most NC families benefit from both. A will controls what happens at your death but goes through probate — a public, court-supervised process. A trust controls assets placed inside it privately, without probate. A pour-over will catches anything you forgot to put in the trust. The combination is the most complete protection. If your estate is modest and uncomplicated, a well-drafted will alone may be sufficient — we'll tell you which makes sense for your situation.
What makes an NC will valid?
Under NC G.S. § 31, a valid will requires: you must be 18+ and of sound mind; the will must be in writing; you must sign it (or direct someone to sign in your presence); and two competent witnesses must sign in your presence. We include a self-proving affidavit under G.S. § 31-11.6, which eliminates the need for witnesses to appear in probate court later.
What is the NC Uniform Trust Code?
The NCUTC (NC G.S. Chapter 36C) is North Carolina's governing statute for trusts. It controls how trusts are created, administered, modified, and terminated in NC. All trusts we draft are NCUTC-compliant — meaning they are valid under NC law and will be recognized by NC courts, financial institutions, and the NC Bar.
Can you include cryptocurrency and digital assets?
Yes. North Carolina adopted RUFADAA (the Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act) which governs how executors and trustees access and manage digital property. We include specific digital asset authority provisions in every trust and will we draft, covering cryptocurrency wallets, exchange accounts, online banking, and social media.
How long does the process take?
From intake to drafted documents is typically 5–10 business days. Execution depends on your schedule — we coordinate witnesses and notary. Most clients have a complete, executed plan within 3 weeks of starting. If you have a time-sensitive situation (upcoming surgery, travel, etc.) tell us and we prioritize.
What happens after I sign — do I need to do anything?
Yes — funding your trust is critical. A trust only controls assets that are titled in its name. We provide a Funding Memorandum explaining exactly which assets to transfer, how to retitle them, and which beneficiary designations to update (retirement accounts, life insurance). An unfunded trust is a common and costly mistake we help you avoid.
